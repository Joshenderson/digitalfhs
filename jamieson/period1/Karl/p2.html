Start of a new Service

The United States Marine Corp was Approved on November 10, 1775 by the Continental Congress, they formed two Marine battalions to fight at sea and on shore. "Resolved, that two Battalions of Marines be raised consisting of one Colonel, two Lieutenant Colonels, two Majors and Officers as usual in other regiments, that they consist of an equal number of privates with other battalions; that particular care be taken that no persons be appointed to office or inlisted into said Battalions, but such as are good seamen, or so acquainted with maritime affairs as to be able to serve to advantage by sea, when required. That they be inlisted and commissioned for and during the present war with Great Britain and the colonies, unless dismissed by Congress. That they be distinguished by the names of the first and second battalions of American Marines, and that they be considered a part of the number, which the continental Army before Boston is ordered to consist of." ----Resolution of the Continental Congress on 10 November 1775

Playing on the World battle field

1901 Marines and Sailors help international forces to quell the Boxer Rebellion. 1918 fighting in the Belleau woods out side of Paris, on June 7, 1918 with only a few grenades and fixed bayonets, Marines charged the German lines and seized enemy positions and wepons in which they used with marksmanship to shread the oncoming German counter attack, after 20 days of intense fighting the Marines won and the Greman survivors gave the Marines the nickname Teufellhuden "Devil Dogs". early 1950's the Marines proved time again how good they are in the U.N Korean war. 1960's and 1970's Marines served in the dreaded jungles of Vietnam. 1991 Operation Desert Storm Marines and U.N forces crush Iraqi forces. 2001 Operation Enduring Freedom begins in Afganistan. 2003 Operation Iraqi Freedom begins and ends the Dictatiorship later the same year

Major Battles

The first amphibious raid that the Marines did was in 1776. It took place in the Bahamas at Fort Nassau, in that fort was weapons supplys that the British stored to use aginst the rebelling thirteen colonies. In 1805 Thomas Jefferson assembled a Marine expeditionary force to deal with the Barbary Coast Pirates, Lieutenant Presley O'Bannon lead his Marines 600 miles across the Libyan Desert to storm the fortified Tripolitan city of Derna to rescue the captured crew of the USS Philadelphia this victory helped Prince Hamet Bey reclaim his throne. 1945 Battle of Iwo Jima, for four days the Marines fought to capture the Japanese airfields on the island, this leading to the symbolic flag rasing on Mt. Suribachi giving a moral boost to the troops below.
