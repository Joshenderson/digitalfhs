Korea Town

Location: Centered on the block of West 32nd Street between Fifth and Sixth Avenues

Korea town serves as a nexus to approximately 218,764 Korean Americans in the New York City Metropolitan Area,the second largest population of ethnic Koreans outside of Korea.The heart of Koreatown is the segment of 32nd Street between Fifth Avenue and Broadway, officially nicknamed "Korea Way". Even though it is only one street long it has more than 100 small businesses on Korea Way

Battery park

Located at the Battery, the southern tip of Manhattan Island in New York City, facing New York Harbor

Battery Park is a public park. The Battery is named for the artillery batteries that were positioned there in the city's early years to protect the settlement behind them. Along the water front Statue Criuses offers ferries to the Statue of liberty and Ellis Island (down below). The Sphere survived the 9/11 attacks of 2001. It was recovered from the rubble pile and represented world peace through world trade The sphere is also considered "a beautiful corpse". It is now displayed in Battery park.

Time square

Located in Midtown Manhattan, New York City, at the junction of Broadway (now converted into a pedestrian plaza) and Seventh Avenue and stretching from West 42nd to West 47th Streets.

Times square is probably one of the best known places of New York City an average of 312,000 pedestrians enter the heart of Times Square each day. Time Square is brightly adorn by LED billboards and advertisements. There are many stores around so you can shop for hours.

Central Park

Central Park stretches from North 110th Street to Central Park South (59th Street), and from Central Park West (8th Avenue) to 5th Ave.

In the heart of Manhattan. It is a national historical landmark that draws in approximately 40 million visitors from all over the world every year.There are lot of activities inside the park:

Chinatown

Located in the borough of Manhattan in New York City, United States, bordering Lower East Side to its east, Little Italy to its north, Civic Center to its south, and Tribeca to its west.

Chinatown is home to the largest enclave of Chinese people in the Western Hemisphere. With an estimated population of 90,000 to 100,000 people, Manhattan's Chinatown is also one of the oldest ethnic Chinese enclaves outside of Asia. In chinatown you can find the most delicious delicacies outside of China.There are also stores that sell the most adorable japanese stationerys for a very low price.

One of the most famous places in China town is Joe's Shanghai Dumpling. They are known for the best soup dumpling outside of China. One bite will get you hooked and you'll just want to keep going back for more.My suggestion would be to go on weekdays because you'll end up waiting 30 mins for a table on weekends.

Joes Shanghai 9 Pell Street New York, New York 10013 Phone: 212-233-8888 Fax: 212-233-0278

Coney island

Located on the Atlantic Ocean in southwestern Brooklyn, New York City.

The site was formerly an outer barrier island, but became partially connected to the mainland by landfill. There are about 3 amusement parks in Coney Island the most notable one is Luna park with the newly made thunderbolt with a 180 degree drop.

Little Italy

Little Italy on Mulberry Street used to extend as far south as Worth Street, as far north as Kenmare St, as far west asLafayette and as far east as Bowery.[1] It is now only three blocks on Mulberry Street

Right next to Chinatown is little Italy. Little Italy was originally like a insular neapolitian village. Over the years the population kept declining. Now it is used as a tourist site with amazing italian food.

Rockefeller Center

located between 48th and 51st streets of Midtown Manhattan, spanning the area between Fifth Avenue and Sixth Avenue

It was declared a National historic landmark in 1987. There is a underground concourse Shopping center. Around the sunken center are flags of all the 50 states of america. the sunken square displays different things every season. In the winter time they have a ice rink and near Christmas they set up a huge Christmas tree.
